    Exit and Entry Administration Law of the PRC

    Chinese name: 中华人民共和国出境入境管理法. The excerpts below are short reference excerpts with editorial English translations; check the official full text for complete wording.

    Not legal advice. Chinese official text prevails. For a case involving penalties, detention, deportation, or a deadline, contact the local exit-entry administration or a qualified lawyer.

    Why foreigners should read this law first

    This law is the anchor for many issues foreigners face in China: accommodation registration, visa/residence deadlines, overstay, illegal employment, exit orders, and deportation.

    Article 39: accommodation registration

    Original excerpt

    外国人在旅馆以外的其他住所居住或者住宿的，应当在入住后二十四小时内由本人或者留宿人，向居住地的公安机关办理登记。

    English reference: If a foreigner stays outside a hotel, the foreigner or host should register with the local public security organ within 24 hours after moving in or arriving.

    Why it matters: This is the legal basis behind private-apartment, friend's-home, and landlord/host registration questions.

    Article 78: illegal residence / overstay

    Original excerpt

    外国人非法居留的，给予警告；情节严重的，处每非法居留一日五百元，总额不超过一万元的罚款或者五日以上十五日以下拘留。

    English reference: Illegal residence may lead to a warning; serious cases may carry a daily fine of RMB 500 up to RMB 10,000 total, or detention for five to fifteen days.

    Why it matters: This is the core article for overstay risk. It does not mean every case is handled the same way; facts, timing, and local handling matter.

    Article 80: illegal employment

    Original excerpt

    外国人非法就业的，处五千元以上二万元以下罚款；情节严重的，处五日以上十五日以下拘留，并处五千元以上二万元以下罚款。

    English reference: Illegal employment by a foreigner can lead to a fine from RMB 5,000 to RMB 20,000; serious cases can include five to fifteen days of detention plus the fine.

    Why it matters: This is why tourist-visa work, wrong-employer work, and informal paid gigs need careful checking before the work starts.
