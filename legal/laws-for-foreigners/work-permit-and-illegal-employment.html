    Work Permit and Illegal Employment

    A source-oriented overview for jobs, internships, teaching, gigs, performances, and consulting in China.

    Home / Legal / Work Permit and Illegal Employment

    Work Authorization Basics

    Do not assume a tourist, transit, student, or family status lets you perform paid work. Work authorization and matching residence status should be verified before starting.

    Exit-Entry Law Article 80

    Original excerpt

    外国人非法就业的，处五千元以上二万元以下罚款；情节严重的，处五日以上十五日以下拘留，并处五千元以上二万元以下罚款。

    English reference: Illegal employment by a foreigner can lead to a fine from RMB 5,000 to RMB 20,000; serious cases can include detention for five to fifteen days plus the fine.

    Employer and intermediary risk

    Original excerpt

    非法聘用外国人的，处每非法聘用一人一万元，总额不超过十万元的罚款；有违法所得的，没收违法所得。

    English reference: Illegally hiring foreigners can lead to a fine calculated per illegally hired person, up to the statutory cap, and illegal gains may be confiscated.

    Common risk signals

    • A company says you can start first and fix the visa later.
    • The work location, employer, or job title differs from the approved documents.
    • A paid teaching, livestreaming, modeling, performance, or consulting gig is described as "just helping."
    • A student internship or part-time role is arranged without school and authority approval where required.
