    The rule foreigners meet during hotels, apartment stays, dorms, and visits with friends.

    Accommodation Registration

    The core national rule is Article 39 of the Exit and Entry Administration Law. The National Immigration Administration also explains online registration for foreigners staying outside hotels.

    Hotel stays

    Original excerpt

    外国人在旅馆住宿的，旅馆应当按照旅馆业治安管理的有关规定为其办理住宿登记。

    English reference: When a foreigner stays in a hotel, the hotel should complete accommodation registration under hotel public-security rules.

    Practical meaning: Give the hotel your passport at check-in. If a hotel refuses foreign guests because it cannot register them, choose another properly registered hotel.

    Non-hotel stays

    Original excerpt

    在旅馆以外的其他住所居住或者住宿的，应当在入住后二十四小时内由本人或者留宿人，向居住地的公安机关办理登记。

    English reference: For non-hotel stays, the foreigner or host should register with the local public security organ within 24 hours after arrival.

    Practical meaning: Ask your friend, landlord, school, or apartment operator whether local registration is done online, at a police station, or through a building/community system.

    Common mistakes

    • Assuming a rental contract automatically equals police registration.
    • Moving apartments and forgetting to update registration.
    • Using a short-term rental where the host cannot explain registration.
