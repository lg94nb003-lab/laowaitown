    Can I work in China on a tourist visa?

    Usually no. Paid work normally requires proper work authorization and the matching stay or residence status.

    Home / Legal / Q&A / Work on tourist visa

    Short answer

    Usually no. Do not start paid work, teaching, performances, consulting, livestreaming, or other commercial activities just because an employer says it is informal.

    Legal basis

    Exit-Entry Administration Law, Article 80

    Original excerpt: 外国人非法就业的，处五千元以上二万元以下罚款；情节严重的，处五日以上十五日以下拘留，并处五千元以上二万元以下罚款。

    English reference: Illegal employment by a foreigner can lead to a fine from RMB 5,000 to RMB 20,000; serious cases can include five to fifteen days of detention plus the fine.

    What this usually means

    China separates tourism, study, family visit, transit, and work purposes. If your visa, stay permit, or residence permit does not authorize work, a paid activity can create illegal-employment risk.

    What you should do

    1. Ask the employer for the work permit process before starting.
    2. Check that the employer, city, job title, and residence status match the approved documents.
    3. If already working without clear authorization, seek qualified advice before leaving China or speaking to authorities.
