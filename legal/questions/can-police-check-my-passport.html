    Can police check my passport in China?

    Foreigners should keep valid identity and stay documents available for lawful checks.

    Home / Legal / Q&A / Passport checks

    Short answer

    Yes, authorities may check identity and stay status in lawful situations. Keep your passport, stay/residence documents, and copies accessible.

    Legal basis

    Exit-Entry Administration Law, Article 38

    Original excerpt: 年满十六周岁的外国人在中国境内停留居留，应当随身携带本人的护照或者其他国际旅行证件，或者外国人停留居留证件，接受公安机关的查验。

    English reference: Foreigners aged 16 or above who stay or reside in China should carry their passport or other travel document, or foreigner stay/residence document, and accept public security checks.

    Practical tips

    • Keep photos of your passport bio page, visa, entry stamp, and residence permit.
    • Hotels, trains, flights, banks, hospitals, and official offices may require the original passport.
    • If your passport is held for a visa process, keep the official receipt.
