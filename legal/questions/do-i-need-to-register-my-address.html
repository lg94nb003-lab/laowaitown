    Legal Q&A

    Do I need to register my address in China?

    Usually yes in some form. Hotels usually handle it; non-hotel stays may require you or your host to register.

    Home / Legal / Q&A / Address registration

    Short answer

    If you stay in a hotel, the hotel normally registers your accommodation. If you stay in a private home or other non-hotel place, the foreigner or host may need to complete registration within 24 hours after arrival.

    Legal basis

    Exit-Entry Administration Law, Article 39

    Original excerpt: 在旅馆以外的其他住所居住或者住宿的，应当在入住后二十四小时内由本人或者留宿人，向居住地的公安机关办理登记。

    English reference: For non-hotel stays, the foreigner or host should register with the local public security organ within 24 hours after arrival.

    Common examples

    • Hotel: usually handled at check-in with your passport.
    • Friend's apartment: ask the host about local police-station or online registration.
    • Rental apartment: ask the landlord or agent before move-in.
