    Legal Q&A

    What happens if I overstay in China?

    Overstay can be treated as illegal residence. Act quickly and contact the local exit-entry administration.

    Home / Legal / Q&A / Overstay

    Short answer

    An overstay can trigger administrative handling. Depending on the facts, consequences may include a warning, fine, detention in serious cases, or future visa/entry problems.

    Legal basis

    Exit-Entry Administration Law, Article 78

    Original excerpt: 外国人非法居留的，给予警告；情节严重的，处每非法居留一日五百元，总额不超过一万元的罚款或者五日以上十五日以下拘留。

    English reference: Illegal residence may lead to a warning; serious cases may carry a daily fine of RMB 500 up to RMB 10,000 total, or detention for five to fifteen days.

    What to do next

    1. Do not ignore the deadline or attempt to leave without understanding your status.
    2. Gather your passport, visa or residence permit, entry stamp, tickets, and explanation documents.
    3. Contact the local public security exit-entry administration as soon as possible.
