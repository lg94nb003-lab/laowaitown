    China Residence Permit 2026: Application Guide

    9 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    If you arrive in China on a Z, X1, S1, Q1, J1, R or K visa and plan to stay longer than the visa's initial 30 days, you must apply for a Residence Permit within 30 days of entry at the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration. Validity is typically 1 year, renewable up to 5 years.

    • ✓ Apply in person at the local PSB Exit-Entry office
    • ✓ Standard processing time: 7–15 working days
    • ✓ Fee: ¥400 single-entry · ¥800 multiple-entry (varies by city)

    Residence Permit vs Visa — what's the difference?

    A visa lets you enter China for a specific purpose. A Residence Permit lets you live in China legally for an extended period, typically replacing the entry visa as your status document.

    • Visa = sticker in your passport, controls entry, usually 30–180 days.
    • Residence Permit = sticker that supersedes the visa once issued; valid 6 months to 5 years; allows multi-entry.
    • Permanent Residence = a separate ID card (Green Card), not the same as Residence Permit. See our Permanent Residence guide.

    Who must apply?

    You must apply for a Residence Permit within 30 days if you hold any of these long-term visas:

    Application process — 4 steps

    1. Complete entry medical exam (Z and X1 holders only). See our medical exam guide. Required before submission.
    2. Register your address with the local police station within 24 hours of arrival. Hotels do this automatically; if you live in a private residence, your landlord or you must register.
    3. Submit application in person at the local PSB Exit-Entry Administration. Many cities now offer online pre-application via the NIA service portal — but final fingerprint capture and document verification still happen on-site.
    4. Collect the permit after 7–15 working days. Some cities offer expedited service in 5 working days for an extra fee.

    Documents checklist

    Bring all originals plus one photocopy of each, unless otherwise noted.

    • Passport with valid visa (Z / X1 / S1 / Q1 / J1 / R / K)
    • Completed application form (download from the NIA service portal or pick up on-site)
    • One recent passport-style photo (white background, 33×48 mm)
    • Police accommodation registration form (issued by local police station)
    • Z visa: work permit notification + employment contract
    • X1 visa: JW201/JW202 form + admission notice from Chinese university
    • S1 / Q1 visa: Notarized proof of family relationship + sponsor's permit/ID
    • K visa: qualification certificates + research/work invitation from Chinese institution
    • Entry medical exam report (Z and X1 only)
    • Proof of address (lease contract or property certificate)

    Document requirements vary slightly by city. Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen and Guangzhou publish detailed checklists on their PSB Exit-Entry Administration websites — verify before your appointment.

    Validity, renewal and changes

    Validity

    • First-time applicants: typically 1 year.
    • Renewing employees with the same employer: 1–5 years depending on contract.
    • Talent / family reunion (R, Q1): up to 5 years.
    • K visa: up to 3 years (per the 2025 implementing rules).

    Renewal

    Apply for renewal at the same PSB office 30 days before expiry. The application is similar to a first-time submission, but some documents (medical exam) may be waived.

    If you change employer, school, or address

    1. New employer/school must reissue work permit or admission documents.
    2. Apply for a new Residence Permit within 10 working days of the change.
    3. Update police accommodation registration if you move house — within 24 hours.

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Missing the 30-day window. Late submission leads to fines and visa-extension complications. Treat day 1 = entry stamp date.
    • Skipping accommodation registration. Even if your hotel registered you, this restarts when you move into a long-term apartment.
    • Forgetting to renew before expiry. A lapsed Residence Permit is treated like an overstay — see our extension & overstay guide.
    • Mixing up "work permit" and "Residence Permit." Work Permit (issued by the Ministry of Human Resources) is the prerequisite for Z visa; Residence Permit is the in-country status document.
    • Trying to apply remotely. Online portals only handle pre-submission; biometrics and final approval still require an in-person appointment.

    Frequently asked questions

    Can I leave China while my Residence Permit application is being processed?

    Generally no — your passport is held at the PSB during processing. If urgent travel is required, request an "in-process exit document" from the PSB. Your application may need to restart on return.

    Does the Residence Permit replace my visa?

    Yes. Once issued, the Residence Permit becomes your status document for entry, exit and stay. The visa underneath becomes inactive but remains in your passport for record.

    Can my family come with me on dependent permits?

    Yes. Spouse and minor children can apply for S1 visas, then convert to Private Affairs (S1-type) Residence Permits aligned with your validity. Notarized marriage / birth certificates are required.

    Can I apply for permanent residence (Green Card) directly with a Residence Permit?

    Holding a Residence Permit is one of the basic conditions, but Permanent Residence has separate criteria (investment, talent, family, contribution). See our Permanent Residence guide.

    Can a visa-free entry convert into a Residence Permit?

    No. Visa-free entry is for short visits up to 30 days and cannot be upgraded inside China. You must exit, obtain the appropriate long-term visa abroad, and then apply for the Residence Permit on re-entry.
