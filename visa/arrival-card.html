    China Arrival Card & Customs Declaration 2026

    6 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    Since November 20, 2025, foreign nationals can — and should — fill in their arrival card online via the NIA portal at s.nia.gov.cn within 24 hours before entry. You'll also need a customs health declaration (also online), and a luggage declaration only if you carry restricted items.

    • ✓ Online filing: open before flight, get a QR code to show at immigration
    • ✓ Paper forms still accepted at most ports as fallback
    • ✓ All three forms are free — beware of third-party sites charging fees

    The three forms you may need

    Three separate documents cover entry, health, and customs. They are run by different agencies and submitted at different counters.

    Filing the online arrival card (since Nov 20, 2025)

    The NIA's online arrival card system s.nia.gov.cn launched on 20 November 2025 as the official preferred channel. Filing is free — beware of look-alike sites that charge "service fees."

    1. Time window: open within 24 hours before your flight to China. Earlier submissions may be rejected.
    2. Information needed: passport biographic data, flight number, China address (hotel name + address suffices), purpose of visit, expected length of stay.
    3. Submit and save: the system generates a QR code (and submission number). Take a screenshot or save the PDF.
    4. At immigration: show the QR code or screenshot to the inspector. Most ports also accept the submission number printed on paper.

    What if I cannot file online?

    Paper arrival cards remain available on aircraft and at the immigration hall. Fill them in fully — incomplete cards delay processing. Some smaller ports (border crossings, sea ports) may still rely primarily on paper.

    Customs health declaration

    Separate from immigration, every traveler must file a health declaration with Customs. The fastest channel is the official Customs mini-program on WeChat or Alipay, searched as "海关旅客指尖服务" (Customs Travelers Fingertip Service).

    • Time window: within 24 hours of entry — earlier submissions expire.
    • Output: a QR health code to scan at the customs channel after immigration.
    • Free: no third-party site needed; ignore look-alike paid services.
    • Paper fallback: the inflight health declaration card remains valid for travelers without smartphones.
    Travelling with a child? Each foreign passenger needs their own arrival card and health declaration — including infants. Use the same parent device to file under each passport.

    Luggage declaration

    Most travelers do not need to file a luggage declaration. You only need it if you carry any of the following items above the duty-free limits:

    • Cash equivalent to USD 5,000 or RMB 20,000 or more.
    • Tobacco products above 400 cigarettes / 100 cigars / 500 g loose tobacco.
    • Alcohol above 1.5 litres of beverages with alcohol content over 12% ABV.
    • Personal belongings of value over RMB 5,000 intended for sale or commercial use.
    • Animal or plant products, currency in unusual quantities, controlled substances, weapons.

    Pick up the paper luggage form on the aircraft (or at the customs hall) and submit it to the customs officer in the red channel. If nothing exceeds limits, walk through the green channel.

    Common filling errors and how to fix them

    • Wrong passport number. Double-check capital letters and zeros vs the letter O. Submit a corrected card on arrival if discovered before going through immigration.
    • Missing China address. A hotel name + city + district is enough; "Sheraton Beijing, Chaoyang District" passes.
    • Stale QR code. The customs health code is valid for only 24 hours from submission, not 24 hours from generation. Refresh on landing day.
    • Submitting too early. Both forms reject entries beyond 24 hours from entry — re-submit closer to your flight.
    • Using a paid third-party site. Only s.nia.gov.cn (immigration) and the official Customs WeChat / Alipay mini-program are free and authoritative.

    Frequently asked questions

    Is online filing mandatory or optional?

    It is the preferred channel since November 20, 2025, and saves time at the border. Paper forms are still accepted as a fallback, but online filing is faster and avoids handwriting errors.

    Do I need a Chinese phone number to file online?

    No. The NIA arrival card portal accepts foreign mobile numbers. The customs mini-program inside WeChat / Alipay also works without a Chinese number, though you must have those apps installed in advance.

    Are connecting passengers in transit required to file?

    Yes if you clear immigration and re-enter, including most 24h / 240h transit visa-free entries. Pure airside transit (no immigration crossing) does not require it, but in practice most international transfers in China require a full entry.

    What about diplomatic and crew passengers?

    Diplomatic and crew passengers follow separate dedicated channels. The standard arrival card is for ordinary foreign passport holders.

    Can I save the QR code offline?

    Yes — take a screenshot. The QR code stays valid as long as the underlying submission has not expired. We recommend printing a backup if you'll have no signal at landing.
