    China Visa-Free Entry 2026: Complete Guide for 50 Countries

    9 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    Ordinary passport holders from 50 countries can enter mainland China visa-free for up to 30 days for tourism, business, family visits or transit — no embassy application, no fees, just board a flight.

    • ✓ No advance application — just show your passport at the border
    • ✓ 30 days per entry, multiple entries allowed within passport validity
    • ✓ Full freedom of movement across mainland China

    Who is eligible?

    China's unilateral visa-free policy is open to ordinary passport holders from 50 specific countries. Diplomatic, service and official passports follow separate bilateral agreements and are not covered here.

    You qualify if all three conditions are met:

    1. You hold an ordinary passport from one of the 50 eligible countries listed below.
    2. Your passport is valid for at least six months on the date of entry and has at least one blank visa page.
    3. The purpose of your visit is tourism, business, family / friend visits, exchange, or transit — and your stay will not exceed 30 days per entry.

    What you can — and cannot — do

    The visa-free entry permit covers short-term private affairs. The scope is broad but not unlimited.

    • Permitted: tourism, sightseeing, visiting family and friends, attending exhibitions, signing contracts, attending business meetings, short conferences, transit to a third destination.
    • Not permitted: paid employment, internships, formal study leading to a degree, news reporting, or any commercial performance. These activities still require the corresponding work (Z), study (X1/X2) or journalist (J1/J2) visa.

    If your trip combines tourism with a few unpaid business meetings, the visa-free entry is fine. The moment money changes hands for your labor, you need a work visa.

    Stay duration and re-entry

    Each entry grants up to 30 days of stay, counted from 00:00 of the day after arrival until midnight of the deadline date written on your entry stamp.

    • Multiple entries allowed within your passport validity — there is no annual cap on how many times you can enter visa-free.
    • Each entry resets the 30-day clock, but officers may question travelers who keep using back-to-back visa-free entries as de-facto long-term residence.
    • If you need to stay longer than 30 days, apply for a 30-day extension at the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration before your deadline expires.

    Entry process at the border

    1. Before boarding: tell your airline at check-in that you are travelling visa-free under the 30-day unilateral policy. Some carriers may otherwise refuse boarding because no Chinese visa is in your passport.
    2. Arrival in China: follow signs to the Foreigner immigration channel. No special queue is needed — visa-free entry uses the normal foreigner lane.
    3. Submit documents: passport, completed arrival card, and (if asked) an onward / return ticket plus your hotel address.
    4. Officer issues a stamp: a 30-day entry stamp valid from 00:00 of the day after arrival. The deadline date is hand-written on the stamp — note it down.
    5. Accommodation registration: within 24 hours of arrival, register your address with the local police. Hotels do this automatically at check-in; if staying with friends or in a short-term rental, you must visit the police station yourself.
    6. Departure: exit through any international port before midnight of your deadline.

    Required documents

    • Valid ordinary passport — at least 6 months remaining validity and one blank page
    • Completed arrival card (paper or self-service kiosk at most ports)
    • Address of your accommodation in China (hotel name + address is enough)
    • Onward / return ticket — not always checked, but officers may request it

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Assuming all 50 countries get identical treatment. The 30-day rule and permitted activities are uniform, but a few countries have shorter pilot periods or are subject to bilateral agreements with different end dates. Always confirm before booking.
    • Counting from the moment of arrival, not midnight. The 30-day clock starts at 00:00 of the day after arrival — you actually get a few extra hours on day one.
    • Skipping accommodation registration. Failing to register with the local police within 24 hours of arrival can result in fines and complications at future entries.
    • Trying to work or attend paid gigs. Visa-free entry is for short-term private affairs only. Paid work, internships, and news reporting require Z, M or J visas.
    • Confusing this with the 240h transit policy. If your country is on the unilateral list (e.g. France, Germany, Australia), use this 30-day visa-free entry — it is more generous than 240h transit and does not require an onward ticket to a third country.

    Frequently asked questions

    Can I extend my stay beyond 30 days?

    Yes. Apply for a 30-day extension at the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration before your stamp expires. Bring your passport, hotel address, and a written reason (continued tourism, family emergency, etc.). Approval is at the officer's discretion and takes 5-7 working days.

    How many times can I enter visa-free in a year?

    There is no fixed annual cap, but officers may scrutinize travelers who use back-to-back 30-day entries as a substitute for residence. If your real purpose is long-term living or working in China, apply for the appropriate residence permit.

    Do I need to book a hotel before arriving?

    You are not formally required to show a hotel booking, but the officer may ask for an address in China. Walk-in hotels are widely available, and most Chinese hotels accept foreign passports and handle the police registration automatically.

    What if I lose my passport during the 30 days?

    Report the loss to the local police immediately, then visit your embassy to apply for an emergency travel document. Take the emergency document and the police report to the Exit-Entry Administration to obtain an exit stamp before leaving China.

    Can I leave mainland China and re-enter visa-free?

    Yes. A trip to Hong Kong, Macau or Taiwan counts as a departure. Re-entering mainland China starts a fresh 30-day clock — but expect officers to ask about the purpose of frequent re-entries.

    Does visa-free entry cover Hong Kong and Macau?

    No. Hong Kong SAR and Macau SAR have their own immigration rules and are not part of mainland China's visa-free policy. Most western passport holders, however, enjoy visa-free entry to HK (up to 90 days) and Macau (up to 30-90 days) under separate bilateral arrangements.
