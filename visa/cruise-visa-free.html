    China Cruise Visa-Free Policy 2026: 15 Days at 13 Ports

    7 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    Foreign passport holders arriving on an international cruise can enter China visa-free for up to 15 days, provided they travel as part of an approved tour group and disembark at one of 13 designated coastal ports. The permit is issued at the border with no fee.

    • ✓ Open to nationals of any country recognised by China
    • ✓ 15 days from arrival, must depart on a cruise from a coastal port
    • ✓ Group tour requirement — organised by an approved travel agency

    Who is eligible?

    Unlike the mainland or Hainan visa-free policies, the cruise policy is not limited to a specific country list. Eligibility is based on the mode of arrival rather than the passport.

    You qualify if:

    1. You arrive on an international cruise ship at one of the 13 designated coastal ports.
    2. You travel as part of an organised tour group arranged by a travel agency registered in China.
    3. You will depart on a cruise from any of the same 13 coastal ports within 15 days.

    Individual cruise passengers who wish to leave the group itinerary should arrange a regular tourism visa in advance.

    Why a tour group is required

    The cruise policy is built on a "group-in, group-out" principle. The cruise line or a Chinese travel agency files an advance passenger manifest with immigration, who then issue a single batch entry permit covering all passengers in the group.

    • The travel agency is responsible for keeping track of passengers and ensuring everyone returns to the ship.
    • If any passenger leaves the group, the agency must report immediately to local police.
    • Independent shore excursions outside the agency itinerary are not covered by the visa-free permit.

    Where you can travel

    Once admitted, the group can travel to coastal cities and inland destinations on the approved itinerary. In practice, common day-trip and multi-day combinations include:

    • Shanghai Wusongkou: Shanghai, Suzhou, Hangzhou, Wuxi, Nanjing.
    • Tianjin: Tianjin, Beijing (including the Forbidden City and Great Wall).
    • Xiamen: Xiamen, Quanzhou, Wuyi Mountain.
    • Guangzhou / Shenzhen: Guangdong cities, Hong Kong day-trips (with re-entry on the same cruise itinerary).
    • Sanya / Haikou: Hainan island highlights — beaches, Boao, Wuzhishan.

    The itinerary is set by the cruise line and travel agency. Free, independent movement outside group activities is not permitted under this policy.

    Disembarkation rules

    1. Before the ship docks: the travel agency submits the passenger manifest and itinerary to the port immigration authority.
    2. On arrival: passengers disembark together; immigration officers stamp a batch entry permit for the group.
    3. During the stay: follow the group itinerary; carry your passport at all times.
    4. Re-boarding or departure: the group exits via any of the 13 cruise ports within 15 days. The agency reconciles the manifest at exit.

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Booking independent shore excursions outside the agency itinerary. Only group activities are covered by the visa-free permit. Independent excursions require a separate tourism visa.
    • Flying out of China after disembarkation. The policy assumes you depart on a cruise from one of the 13 coastal ports — not on an international flight. If you wish to fly out, apply for a regular visa in advance.
    • Overstaying the 15-day cap. Returning to the ship late or skipping departure can result in fines and a future entry record.
    • Confusing cruise visa-free with Hainan visa-free. A cruise calling at Sanya uses the cruise policy (group, 15 days). Flying directly to Sanya uses the Hainan policy (individual, 30 days).
    • Assuming all coastal ports are eligible. Only the 13 designated cruise terminals listed above qualify. Smaller fishing or freight ports do not.

    Frequently asked questions

    Can I leave the group and travel independently?

    No. The cruise visa-free permit is issued as a group permit. Leaving the group itinerary is treated as a breach of conditions and can lead to fines and a future entry record for both you and the travel agency.

    Can I fly out of China at the end of the cruise?

    No. The policy assumes you depart on a cruise from one of the 13 ports. If you wish to disembark in China and fly out, you need a regular tourism (L) visa instead.

    Does my nationality need to be on a specific list?

    No. The cruise policy is open to all nationalities recognised by Chinese immigration. The eligibility hinges on arriving by international cruise as part of an approved tour group.

    Can children travel under the cruise visa-free policy?

    Yes. Minors travelling with parents or legal guardians are included in the group manifest and receive the same 15-day permit.

    What happens if the ship is delayed or the itinerary changes?

    The travel agency notifies immigration of itinerary changes. Minor delays within the 15-day window are usually accommodated. Major changes that extend the stay beyond 15 days require formal notification and may require additional documentation.

    Can I combine the cruise policy with another visa-free policy?

    Generally no. Each entry uses one policy. If your nationality qualifies for the 30-day unilateral or 240-hour transit policy and you want independent travel, those policies are usually more flexible than the group-based cruise option.
