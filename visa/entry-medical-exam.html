    China Entry Medical Exam 2026: Required Tests, Cost & ITHC Booking

    10 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    If you plan to live in China longer than 6 months on a Z, X1, Q1, S1, J1, R or K visa, you must complete a foreigner physical examination before applying for your Residence Permit. Take it at any International Travel Healthcare Center (ITHC) — operated nationwide by China Customs.

    • ✓ Cost: ¥500–700 (varies by city)
    • ✓ Time required: ~2 hours on-site; report ready in 3–5 working days
    • ✓ Report validity: 6 months from issue date

    Who must take the entry medical exam?

    Per Frontier Health and Quarantine Law of the People's Republic of China and the Regulations on Health Examination of Foreigners, you are required to undergo a physical examination if you are applying to:

    • Reside in China permanently (Permanent Residence ID Card applicants)
    • Stay for more than one year for work, study, family reunion, or research
    • Convert a Z / X1 / Q1 / S1 / J1 / R / K visa into a Residence Permit

    Holders of short-term visas (L, M, F, X2, S2, Q2, J2, G, C) and visa-free entrants do not need this exam.

    What's tested — full list

    The standard foreigner physical examination ("Foreigner Physical Examination Form" 外国人体格检查记录) covers the following items. The form template is published by the Chinese embassy and ITHC.

    CategoryItemsPurpose

    Internal medicineHeart, lungs, abdomen, lymph nodesHeart/lung disease screen

    Pregnant women: chest X-ray is replaced with alternative screening — inform staff in advance.

    How to take the exam — 5 steps

    1. Book online. Use the WeChat official account "海关总署国际旅行卫生保健中心" (GAC International Travel Healthcare Center) or the city ITHC website (e.g. beijingithc.org.cn, sh.ithc.cn). English booking is available in major cities.
    2. Prepare materials. Bring your passport, 4 white-background 2-inch photos (4.8 × 3.3 cm), and exam fee in cash or via mobile payment. Fast overnight before the exam (no food / water for at least 8 hours).
    3. Register on-site. Arrive 15 minutes early. The center provides barcode-tracked stations; staff will guide you through each room in sequence.
    4. Complete all stations in roughly 2 hours. The intelligent triage system reduces queue time at peak periods.
    5. Collect the report. Reports are typically ready in 3–5 working days. Many centers now offer e-reports via WeChat with QR validation; the paper report bears the ITHC's red official seal.

    Used an overseas medical exam? Here's how to validate it

    If you completed a physical exam in your home country before traveling to China:

    • The exam must be done in a public hospital within 6 months before entering China.
    • Use the official "Foreigner Physical Examination Form" (PDF available from any Chinese embassy/consulate website, e.g. ie.china-embassy.gov.cn, dated 2025-11). The form must include your photo affixed and stamped with the hospital seal.
    • Bring all original test reports (chest X-ray film, ECG strip, blood test printouts) attached to the form.
    • After arrival, you must take everything to a Chinese ITHC for verification and seal. The ITHC reviews completeness and either stamps approval or requests missing tests to be redone locally.
    • Verification fee is roughly ¥200–400; redoing missing tests is charged per item.

    Common reasons reports get rejected: missing chest X-ray film, missing HIV/syphilis blood markers, undated form, no hospital seal, language other than English/Chinese without certified translation.

    Already booked your exam? Plan the next step. Once you have the report, you have 30 days from entry to apply for the Residence Permit.

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Eating breakfast. Blood and abdominal ultrasound require fasting 8+ hours. Some centers will reschedule you, others let you wait until afternoon.
    • Wearing metal accessories. Underwire bras, necklaces and metal-button shirts can interfere with chest X-ray; you'll be asked to remove them.
    • Forgetting photos. Centers do offer on-site photo machines but lines can be long during peak hours.
    • Taking the report directly to the PSB. Overseas reports must first go to the ITHC for the verification seal — without it, the PSB will not accept it.
    • Using a private clinic. Only China Customs ITHCs and a few designated hospitals can issue the legally accepted report. A normal hospital health check is not equivalent.

    Frequently asked questions

    Will I be deported if my HIV / hepatitis B test is positive?

    Since 2010, HIV-positive status is no longer an automatic bar to entry, and hepatitis B has never been one. Decisions are made case by case under the Frontier Health Quarantine framework; many positive applicants successfully obtain Residence Permits. Consult the local ITHC and PSB for guidance.

    Is the report valid for visa applications outside China?

    The Chinese-format report is recognized by Chinese authorities only. For other-country visa medicals (US, Australia, NZ, Korea), book a designated visa-medical appointment via the same ITHC system but on a different track.

    Do my children need the exam?

    Children under 18 applying for an X1 or S1 long-term residence permit follow a reduced-test version of the same form. Chest X-ray is usually waived for children under 14.

    How long is the report valid?

    The report is valid for 6 months from issue. If your Residence Permit application is delayed past that window, you must redo the relevant tests.

    Can I take the exam in any city, then submit in another?

    Yes. The ITHC report is recognized nationwide. However, some PSB offices prefer a local report — ask before booking if you plan to file in a different city.
