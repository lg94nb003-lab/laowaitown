    China Visa-Free Entry for Spain Citizens 2026

    Quick answer for Spanish citizens: the 30-day visa-free route may be available, 240-hour transit may be available, 24-hour transit is generally possible when transit conditions are met, and Hainan visa-free entry may be available.

    Who this page is for

    This page is for travelers holding an ordinary Spain passport who want a fast first check before booking flights or hotels. It is not a substitute for official advice from the airline, Chinese embassy, consulate, or border inspection authority.

    Conditions checklist

    • Your passport must be valid and match your booking details.
    • Your purpose must fit the specific exemption route you plan to use.
    • For transit, your route must continue to a third country or region, with confirmed onward transport.
    • For Hainan, your activity area is normally limited to Hainan Province.
    • Keep hotel booking, itinerary, and return or onward ticket ready for inspection.

    Common mistakes

    • Counting the stay from the wrong date.
    • Assuming 240-hour transit allows travel anywhere in China.
    • Booking a round trip that is not actually a third-country transit.
    • Arriving without hotel address, onward ticket, or a clear itinerary.

    FAQ

    Can Spanish citizens enter China without a visa in 2026?

    Spanish citizens are listed in LAOWAITOWN's current 30-day visa-free dataset for ordinary passports, subject to purpose and stay limits.

    Can Spanish citizens use China's 240-hour transit policy?

    Yes, LAOWAITOWN's current dataset includes Spain in the 240-hour transit country list, provided the route, port, documents, and onward-ticket conditions are met.

    Can Spanish citizens use Hainan's 30-day visa-free policy?

    Yes, LAOWAITOWN's current Hainan dataset includes Spain, but travel is limited to Hainan and subject to the official Hainan policy conditions.
