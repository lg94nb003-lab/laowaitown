    China Visa Extension & Overstay 2026: Process, Fines & How to Avoid Trouble

    11 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    Apply for a visa extension at the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration (PSB EEA) at least 7 days before your current stay expires. Most ordinary visas can be extended once for up to the original duration. Overstaying triggers a fine of ¥500 per day, capped at ¥10,000; overstays beyond 15 days may add 5–15 days detention.

    • ✓ Extension fee: ¥160 (most nationalities; varies)
    • ✓ Processing time: up to 7 working days; passport stays at PSB
    • ✓ Legal basis: 2025 revised Exit-Entry Administration Regulations (State Council, 2025-08-14)

    Who can extend a visa?

    Most ordinary visa categories can be extended once on legitimate grounds. Eligibility is decided case-by-case by the local PSB EEA.

    VisaTypical extensionCommon grounds
    L (tourist)Up to 30 days, onceContinued tourism, illness, force majeure
    M (business)Up to 30–60 days, onceContinuing negotiations / project
    F (exchange)Up to original durationExtended program from inviter
    X2 (short study)Up to original durationSchool-issued extension letter
    Q2 / S2 (private affairs)Up to 180 days totalFamily / personal matters
    Z / X1 / Q1 / S1 / J1 / R / K (long-term)Cannot be extended; convert to Residence Permit instead—
    Visa-free entry / 240h transitCannot be extendedMust exit before stay expires

    Per the 2025 revised Article 21, extension can be denied if the applicant fails to provide required materials, has prior overstay records, or is otherwise deemed inadmissible.

    Extension process — 5 steps

    1. Decide your timing. Apply at least 7 days before your current stay expires; some cities accept up to 30 days early.
    2. Gather documents (see checklist below). Make photocopies — most PSB counters keep them.
    3. Visit the PSB EEA in the city where you are staying. Walk-in is usually allowed; major cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen) recommend booking via the NIA service portal (s.nia.gov.cn).
    4. Submit and pay. The standard fee is ¥160 per extension, payable on submission. The PSB retains your passport during processing.
    5. Collect your passport with the new extension stamp/sticker after up to 7 working days. The new stay starts the day after the original stay would have expired.

    Documents checklist

    • Passport with the current valid visa / stamp
    • Completed Visa / Stay / Residence Application Form (Form V.2013, PSB version)
    • One recent passport-style photo (33×48 mm, white background)
    • Proof of accommodation registration from local police
    • Proof of extension reason:
      • Tourist (L): hotel bookings, return ticket, sufficient funds proof
      • Business (M): updated invitation letter from Chinese counterpart
      • Family (Q2/S2): notarized relationship documents + sponsor's ID
      • Medical: hospital certificate showing inability to travel
    • Proof of return / onward travel (return air ticket beyond extended stay)
    Holding a long-term visa? Z / X1 / Q1 / S1 / J1 / R / K visas don't get "extended" — they convert to a Residence Permit.

    Overstay — fines, detention, future-entry impact

    Per Article 78 of the Exit and Entry Administration Law and the 2025 revised Regulations, overstaying any visa, stay sticker, or Residence Permit constitutes illegal residence.

    Overstay durationPenalty
    1 – 15 days¥500 per day fine; warning issued
    Cumulative cap¥10,000 maximum monetary fine
    16+ days, or repeat offenses, or refused to leave¥500/day fine + 5–15 days administrative detention
    Severe / fraudulent casesDetention + deportation + multi-year re-entry ban (typically 1–10 years)

    You must report any overstay within 24 hours of discovery to the local PSB. A self-reported short overstay (1–2 days, with valid reason) is often handled with the minimum fine and no detention.

    Already overstayed? Step-by-step recovery

    1. Stop traveling and stay where you are. Do not attempt to leave through the airport — Border Inspection will detect the overstay automatically.
    2. Visit the local PSB EEA within 24 hours. Bring passport, photos, accommodation registration.
    3. File a written explanation describing the reason and timeline. Officers issue an Administrative Penalty Decision.
    4. Pay the fine at a designated bank within the deadline on the decision (usually 15 days). Keep the receipt.
    5. Apply for an exit visa or stay sticker with the new "departure within X days" notation. You typically receive 7–15 days to leave the country.
    6. Leave by the new deadline through any open port. Border Inspection will record the case.

    If detained, you can request consular notification to your embassy. Foreign nationals are entitled to consular access under the Vienna Convention.

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Counting from visa expiry instead of stay expiry. The "Duration of Each Stay" stamp is what counts, not the visa's "Enter Before" date.
    • Booking the extension trip too early. Some PSBs reject extensions filed more than 30 days before expiry.
    • Trying to extend a visa-free stay. Visa-free entries (15 / 30 / 60 / 90 days), 240-hour transit, and Hainan visa-free cannot be extended — you must exit and re-enter.
    • Assuming "weekend" = "no overstay." Day 1 of overstay starts at 00:00 the day after expiry; weekends and holidays count.
    • Believing overstays are forgiven if you "just leave." Border Inspection issues an automatic fine + ban risk; voluntary departure is no excuse.

    Frequently asked questions

    How many times can I extend a tourist visa?

    Generally once per visa, for up to 30 days. Repeated extensions are rarely approved; PSB officers expect you to leave and apply for a new visa abroad.

    Will an overstay affect future Chinese visa applications?

    Yes. Overstays — even small ones — are recorded in the NIA database and disclosed during future visa interviews. Multi-day or repeat overstays often trigger refusals or 1–10 year entry bans.

    Can I leave through Hong Kong or Macao to "reset"?

    No. Departing to HK / Macao still goes through mainland Border Inspection and overstay penalties apply. HK and Macao have their own immigration systems and visa rules.

    What if I overstayed because I was hospitalized?

    Bring a hospital certificate. Force-majeure overstays usually result in waived fines, but you still must report within 24 hours and obtain a corrected exit document.

    Where do I find my "Duration of Each Stay"?

    It's printed on the visa sticker (e.g. "30 days") for each entry, or stamped at the port of entry as "Enter Before" + "Duration." For visa-free entries, the entry stamp shows the latest legal stay date.
