    240-Hour Transit Visa-Free Policy 2026

    8 min read By LAOWAITOWN Editorial

    Quick Answer

    Citizens of 55 countries can transit through China visa-free for up to 240 hours (10 days) via any of 65 ports across 24 provinces, provided they hold a confirmed onward ticket to a third country or region.

    • ✓ No application before travel — granted at the border
    • ✓ Cross-province movement allowed (within designated zones)
    • ✓ Tourism, business, family visits are permitted

    Who is eligible?

    The 240-hour transit visa-free policy is open to ordinary passport holders of 55 specifically designated countries. The list covers most of Europe, North America, Oceania, and selected partners in Asia and Latin America.

    The 55 eligible countries

    Europe (40): Albania, Austria, Belarus, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Russia, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Ukraine, the United Kingdom.

    Americas (6): United States, Canada, Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Chile.

    Oceania (2): Australia, New Zealand.

    Asia (7): South Korea, Japan, Singapore, Brunei, the United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Indonesia.

    Three core conditions

    1. You hold an ordinary passport from one of the 55 countries (diplomatic and service passports follow separate rules).
    2. You are transiting to a third country or region — your final destination cannot be the same country you departed from.
    3. You hold a confirmed onward ticket with a fixed date and seat, departing within 240 hours of your arrival in China.

    Eligible ports (65 across 24 provinces)

    You must enter and exit through a designated port. As of 2026, China has expanded the list to 65 air, sea, rail, and road ports across 24 provinces and municipalities.     Where you can travel during your stay

    Movement is allowed across most of mainland China. The 24 eligible provinces and municipalities are grouped into regional zones for free intra-zone movement. You may also fly between zones, as long as your final exit is from one of the 65 designated ports.

    • Permitted activities: tourism, sightseeing, family visits, attending business meetings, signing contracts, attending exhibitions and trade fairs.
    • Not permitted: employment, study leading to a degree, news reporting (these still require the corresponding visa class — Z, X1/X2, J1/J2).

    How to apply at the border

    The 240-hour transit visa-free permit is issued on arrival. There is no advance application, no embassy appointment, and no fee.

    1. Before boarding: tell your airline at check-in that you will use 240-hour transit visa-free entry. Some airlines may otherwise refuse boarding because no Chinese visa is in your passport.
    2. Arrival in China: follow the signs to the Foreigner immigration channel. Look for a desk marked "Temporary Entry Permit" or ask an officer for the 240-hour transit lane.
    3. Submit documents: passport, onward ticket to a third country, and a completed arrival card.
    4. Officer issues a stamp: a temporary entry permit valid up to 240 hours from 00:00 of the day after arrival. The officer will write your latest legal exit date — keep this date in mind.
    5. During your stay: if you change accommodation in China, register with the local police within 24 hours (or your hotel does this automatically at check-in).
    6. Departure: exit via any of the 65 designated ports before midnight of your latest legal date.

    Required documents

    • Valid ordinary passport with at least 6 months remaining and one blank page
    • Confirmed onward ticket to a third country or region (paper or e-ticket)
    • Completed arrival card (paper at most ports; some now offer self-service kiosks)
    • Address of your accommodation in China (hotel name + address is enough)

    Common mistakes to avoid

    • Using the wrong third country. Returning to your country of departure does not count as transit. Example: Tokyo → Beijing → Tokyo is not eligible. Tokyo → Beijing → Seoul is eligible.
    • Counting from arrival time, not midnight. The 240 hours start at 00:00 of the day after you arrive — you actually get a few extra hours, but never less.
    • Exiting from a non-designated port. Even if your stay is under 240 hours, exit must be from one of the 65 designated ports.
    • No accommodation registration. Foreigners must register with the local police within 24 hours of arrival (hotels do it for you; if staying with friends, you must visit the police station yourself).
    • Confusing 240h with 30-day visa-free entry. If your country is on the unilateral 30-day list (e.g. France, Germany, Australia), you usually do not need 240h transit — the 30-day rule is more flexible. Use our checker to confirm.

    Frequently asked questions

    Can I leave the city or province during 240-hour transit?

    Yes. As of 2024, movement across all 24 designated provinces and municipalities is allowed. Earlier versions of the policy restricted movement to a single zone, but this is no longer the case.

    Can my country of origin and the third country be the same continent?

    Yes. The only requirement is that the third country is different from your country of departure. For example, Paris → Beijing → Seoul is fine, even though both Paris and Seoul are not in mainland China.

    What if my onward flight is delayed and I overstay the 240 hours?

    Contact the local Public Security Bureau Exit-Entry Administration before your deadline expires. With proof of force majeure (cancelled flights, illness with hospital records), they typically grant a free extension. Overstaying without notification can lead to fines of up to ¥10,000 and an entry ban.

    Does Hong Kong, Macau or Taiwan count as a "third country/region"?

    Yes. Hong Kong SAR, Macau SAR and Taiwan are all treated as separate "regions" for transit purposes, so itineraries like Bangkok → Shanghai → Hong Kong qualify.

    Can I apply for 240h transit if I have a valid Chinese visa?

    You can, but it is unnecessary. With a valid visa you simply enter using the visa, which gives you the full duration of stay printed on the visa. The 240h policy is mainly for travelers without a Chinese visa.

    Can I work or attend a paid conference under 240h transit?

    Short business meetings, signing contracts and attending unpaid exhibitions are allowed. Paid work, employment, internships, or formal study are not allowed — these require Z, M or X visas.
